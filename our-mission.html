    Our mission

    Approach and Philosophies

    Approach and Philosophies

    Our main mission is to support our guests to achieve their goals as independently as possible during their holiday with us. We encourage our guests to embrace their white-water rafting trip with excitement, we hope they learn those dance moves that they want to share with everyone and we join in their pleasure of photographing the stunning scenery in which we are immersed. To foster that environment on our holidays, we undertake certain philosophies that are very simple.

    Respect

    Only support where needed; we will never be intrusive with our support of a guest. Many people with good intentions offer support which, in the long run, doesn’t help the guest to progress. We treat each guest as an equal to ourselves and just because they need support in certain areas, doesn’t mean they should be talked down to. We all need support in our lives; many of us require support around car mechanics as we have limited knowledge in that area, therefore we pay someone to support us with this, but do not expect to be patronised when dealing with a mechanic. We endeavour to give as much time as we can to each guest and listen a lot. This is valuing people; valuing someone’s dreams, stories and futures. We can aid in facilitating dreams as well as helping heal a hurt, just by listening.

    Organisational

    Our holidays are pitched at a level that will be appealing to anyone. Younger guests today want the same experiences as their peers, like trekking in the mountains like their older siblings, they want to see the Northern Lights in the land of fire and ice, just like their friends from school did in their gap year and they want to experience the longest train ride in the world that crosses the Siberian plains like they saw in a recent travel documentary on television. We want them to experience this too.

    As a team of staff we are always flexible and adapt to changes we may have to make to accommodate someone’s needs that were not planned for. For example, someone who at the last minute may decide that they do not wish to participate in an activity, like kayaking. This can be literally as they are about to step into the kayak itself.

    We pride ourselves on clear and regular communication between us and the care network of our guests, to be able to ascertain the best care plan and risk assessment for the holiday. This will help to create the best holiday possible that is fun, challenging, progressive but safe, a very important element if we are to be trusted by the guest and their care network.
